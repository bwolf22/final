Breaking the Academic Marathon: UMD Contemplates Fall Break for Stress Relief


Dateline: May 9, 2024

By: Ben Wolf

College Park — Providing a fall break has been a yearly debate at the University of Maryland, but that debate might close as the school has discussed provisionary measures for implementing one.

On the UMD academic calendar, the school does not give students any days off from Labor Day until Thanksgiving, a continuous 12-week stretch of classes. Students feel that this long stretch intensifies their mental strain, making the fall semester more difficult.

After conducting a poll of 100 students at UMD, the results showed that 55 percent of the survey respondents said that they performed better academically during the spring semester, which features a break, compared to the academic marathon in the fall semester.

“The three months without a break is a lot and gets tiring, and I think we need a break so students can be more mentally prepared for finals,” said freshman finance major Ryan Epstein.

The University of Maryland Senate last discussed fall break in February. That meeting examined the calendar changes the school must make to provide time off. Since that meeting, the University Senate has initiated a discussion with the University System of Maryland Chancellor's office regarding the necessary schedule changes.

The spots that the school was considering placing the two days off were on the Monday and Tuesday before Thanksgiving, Election Day, or mid-October. Ultimately, mid-October was the best option to break up the semester.

“The Monday/Tuesday before Thanksgiving is so late in the semester that it doesn't really accomplish the goal of providing stress relief in the middle of the term,” said Professor and Associate Provost Betsy Beise.

The two days in October will be “no-class” days rather than university closures or holidays, so employees must report to work on those days.

Despite the break not being a university closure, there are loopholes the faculty can use to enjoy the time off. “Employees on 12-month contracts accumulate administrative leave and could use that during those days,” said Beise. “Faculty on nine-month contracts do not accumulate leave, so they could not do so, but the break from delivering instruction would be a break for them. ”

With mental health concerns rising at UMD due to two on-campus suicides and spiking anxiety rates, according to the Diamondback and University Senate, students are pushing to have some extra time to decompress, spend time with friends and family, and get situated to close out the semester strong.

“I’m from South Florida, so I don’t get to go home and see my parents often,” said sophomore government and politics major Noah Kopelowitz. “So I would definitely use the fall break to catch up with my family and take a moment to appreciate things in life that aren’t academic based. ”

Of the 14 Big Ten Schools during the 2023-2024 academic year, UMD, Rutgers, Penn State, Michigan State, Illinois, Northwestern, Wisconsin, Iowa, and Minnesota do not provide a break during the fall semester. Ohio State, Michigan, Indiana, Purdue, and Nebraska give their students two days off in October.

While less than 50 percent of the Big Ten provides a fall Break, this differs for the Delaware, Maryland, and Virginia (DMV) schools. American University, George Washington University, Georgetown, and the University of Virginia are schools that provide a fall break in October. The University of Delaware cancels classes for Election Day at the beginning of November.

“It frustrates me when I know that all of my friends are at home hanging out with each other and their families while I’m sitting at my desk doing homework,” said Epstein.

To implement a fall break, the school must adhere to the state's instructional day requirement, adding two days to the academic calendar.

“This is pretty disruptive, and it would mean starting earlier in the late summer and changing our summer session dates and, in some years, spring semesters,” said Beise. “The state regulations are more strict than the federal requirements, so one possible solution is to try to get the state regulations more flexible.”

The earliest UMD students and staff could see a fall break is the fall 2025 semester, and the school must make a final decision before the fall 2024 semester for that to happen.
